Quadrature Mirror Filter (QMF)

A special type of two-channel filter bank where the high-pass filter’s frequency response is the mirror image of the low-pass filter’s response around π/2.

Answer:

A QMF is a pair of filters where the high-pass filter is the frequency mirror of the low-pass filter about π/2. Used in subband coding to split the signal into low- and high-frequency bands, enabling efficient coding and compression.

Answer:

If H0(ejω) is low-pass, then H1(ejω) = H0(ej(π−ω)) is its high-pass mirror. This symmetry allows splitting the spectrum into two complementary halves.
