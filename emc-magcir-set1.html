Answer:

An electric circuit is a path for the flow of electric current (carried by electrons). A magnetic circuit is a path for magnetic flux (lines of force).

Key differences:

  • Electric circuit: Continuous flow of charge; energy is dissipated (e.g., heat in resistors).
  • Magnetic circuit: No particles flow; magnetic flux is established; energy is stored in the field once flux is established.

Fringing is the spreading of magnetic flux lines at an air gap.

Flux lines spread into surrounding air, increasing effective cross-sectional area and decreasing reluctance of the gap. Must be considered for accurate calculations in motors and actuators.

Reluctance (‚Ñú) is the opposition a magnetic material offers to the formation of magnetic flux. A material with low reluctance, like iron, allows magnetic flux to pass easily, while a material with high reluctance, like air, resists the flux.

Analogy to Resistance: Just as resistance opposes electric current in a circuit, reluctance opposes magnetic flux in a magnetic circuit. Both determine how easily the respective "flow" (current or flux) can pass through a material.
