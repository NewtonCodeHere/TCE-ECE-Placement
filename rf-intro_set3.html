Microwave sources generate high-frequency signals used in radar, satellite, mobile, and Wi-Fi systems. They provide:

Stable frequency generation (e.g., Gunn diodes, klystrons, magnetrons).

High bandwidth capacity for data communication.

Line-of-sight propagation, suitable for wireless links.

Without microwave sources, long-distance and high-speed wireless communication would not be possible.

A waveguide is a hollow metallic structure that guides electromagnetic waves.

At microwave frequencies, conventional wires or coaxial cables suffer high losses.

Waveguides provide low attenuation, high power handling, and minimal interference.

They support TE and TM modes, making them ideal for radar and satellite applications.
