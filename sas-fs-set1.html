SAS - Continuous-Time Fourier Series - Set 1

Answer:

🌟 A periodic signal is one that repeats itself after a fixed interval of time T.

🌟 Mathematically, x(t) = x(t + T) for all t.

🌟 The smallest positive value of T is called the fundamental period.

🌟 Examples include sinusoidal, square, and sawtooth signals.

Answer:

🌟 Fourier series expresses a periodic signal as a weighted sum of sinusoids.

🌟 It uses sine and cosine terms at integer multiples of the fundamental frequency.

🌟 The coefficients capture the signal’s frequency-domain content.

🌟 It provides a bridge between time-domain and frequency-domain analysis.

Answer:

🌟 Fundamental frequency is the reciprocal of the fundamental period f0 = 1/T.

🌟 It is the lowest frequency present in a periodic signal.

🌟 Higher harmonics are integer multiples of this frequency.

🌟 It determines the base oscillation cycle of the signal.

Answer:

🌟 Harmonics are sinusoidal components with frequencies that are integer multiples of the fundamental frequency.

🌟 The n-th harmonic has frequency n f0.

🌟 Each harmonic contributes to the signal’s shape.

🌟 The amplitude and phase of harmonics define waveform characteristics.

Answer:

🌟 Fourier coefficients quantify the amplitude and phase of sinusoidal components.

🌟 They are obtained by integrating the signal over one period.

🌟 Coefficients include DC component, cosine, and sine terms.

🌟 They provide compact representation of the signal’s spectrum.

Answer:

🌟 A signal is even if x(t) = x(−t).

🌟 A signal is odd if x(t) = −x(−t).

🌟 Even signals have only cosine terms in Fourier series.

🌟 Odd signals have only sine terms in Fourier series.

Answer:

🌟 The DC component is the average value of the signal over one period.

🌟 It is given by the Fourier coefficient a0/2.

🌟 It represents the zero-frequency component of the signal.

🌟 It shifts the signal vertically in the time domain.

Answer:

🌟 Parseval’s theorem relates total signal power to Fourier coefficients.

🌟 The average power of a signal equals the sum of squared magnitudes of coefficients.

🌟 It provides an energy distribution across harmonics.

🌟 It is useful in power spectrum analysis.
