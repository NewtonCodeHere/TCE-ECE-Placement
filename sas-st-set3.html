Answer:

🌟 A band-limited signal is one whose frequency content is confined within a finite range.

🌟 Maximum frequency is fmax.

🌟 Such signals can be perfectly reconstructed if sampled at ≥ Nyquist rate.

🌟 Non-band-limited signals cannot be perfectly sampled without loss.

Answer:

🌟 Ideal reconstruction uses sinc interpolation, but it requires infinite time support.

🌟 In practice, reconstruction uses approximation filters.

🌟 Common filters: Butterworth, Chebyshev, or digital FIR filters.

🌟 The goal is to closely approximate the original signal.

Answer:

🌟 Discrete-time representation is obtained by sampling a continuous signal at fixed intervals.

🌟 Each sample represents the signal value at that instant.

🌟 Mathematically: x[n] = x(nTs), where Ts is sampling interval.

🌟 It is used in digital systems for storage and processing.

Answer:

🌟 Quantization is the process of mapping sampled values into discrete amplitude levels.

🌟 It introduces quantization error/noise due to approximation.

🌟 Quantization follows after sampling in digital signal processing.

🌟 Higher quantization levels give better accuracy.

Answer:

🌟 In frequency domain, aliasing appears as spectral overlap.

🌟 The replicated spectra due to sampling overlap when fs < 2fmax.

🌟 Overlap results in distortion and loss of original frequency content.

🌟 Anti-aliasing filter eliminates higher frequencies to avoid overlap.
