SAS - Z Transform - Set 1

Answer:

🌟 The z-Transform is a mathematical tool used to represent discrete-time signals in the complex frequency domain.

🌟 It is defined as X(z) = ∑n=−∞∞ x[n]z−n.

🌟 It generalizes the Discrete-Time Fourier Transform (DTFT) by allowing complex values of z.

🌟 It is essential for analyzing stability, causality, and system behavior.

Answer:

🌟 ROC is the set of values of z for which the z-Transform series converges.

🌟 It depends on the type of sequence (causal, anti-causal, finite-duration).

🌟 For a stable system, ROC includes the unit circle |z| = 1.

🌟 ROC is crucial in uniquely identifying signals from their z-Transform.
