Answer:

🌟 Frequency response is the response of the system to complex exponential inputs ejωn.

🌟 It is given by H(ejω) = ∑ h[n]e−jωn.

🌟 It describes how system amplitude and phase vary with frequency.

🌟 It helps analyze filtering properties of the system.

Answer:

🌟 Magnitude response is |H(ejω)|, the absolute value of the frequency response.

🌟 It represents gain applied to input signals at different frequencies.

🌟 Determines passband and stopband characteristics.

🌟 Critical in filter design applications.

Answer:

🌟 Phase response is ∠H(ejω).

🌟 It shows phase shift introduced at different frequencies.

🌟 Linear phase ensures no distortion in waveform shape.

🌟 Phase characteristics are crucial for communication systems.

🌟 The location of poles relative to the unit circle determines system stability.

🌟 Poles near the unit circle cause resonance and high gain at certain frequencies.

🌟 Stable systems require poles inside the unit circle.

🌟 Frequency response peaks correspond to pole proximity to unit circle.

🌟 Zeros represent frequencies where system response is attenuated.

🌟 If a zero lies on the unit circle, the response is zero at that frequency.

🌟 Placement of zeros shapes the stopband of filters.

🌟 Zeros influence both magnitude and phase response.

🌟 It represents the system in algebraic form.

🌟 Poles and zeros of H(z) define the system’s behavior.

🌟 Frequency response is obtained by evaluating H(z) on unit circle z = ejω.
