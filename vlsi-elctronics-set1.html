A CMOS inverter is a basic logic gate made using a PMOS and an NMOS transistor. When the input is high (logic 1), the NMOS conducts and PMOS is off, giving output low (logic 0). When the input is low (logic 0), the PMOS conducts and NMOS is off, giving output high (logic 1).

Answer:

The DC transfer characteristic shows the relationship between the input voltage (Vin) and output voltage (Vout) of the inverter. It helps analyze noise margins, switching threshold (Vth), and logic levels. The curve transitions sharply from high to low output around the threshold voltage.

The switching threshold (Vth) is the input voltage at which the output voltage is equal to the input (Vin = Vout). Ideally, it's VDD/2, providing equal noise margins for logic high and logic low.

Logical effort is a method to estimate and compare the delay of different logic gates relative to an inverter. It helps in optimizing circuit speed by considering both gate complexity and load.

Answer:

The logical effort of a CMOS inverter is 1 (baseline). Other gates like NAND or NOR have higher logical efforts, meaning more delay compared to an inverter for the same load.
