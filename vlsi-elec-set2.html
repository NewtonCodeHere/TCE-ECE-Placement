FET RC model- Set 2

Answer:

The FET RC model is a simplified representation of a Field Effect Transistor (FET) using resistors (R) and capacitors (C) to capture the resistive and capacitive parasitics inherent in the device.
In VLSI, this model is essential for analyzing timing, signal delay, and power consumption, especially as device dimensions shrink and parasitic effects dominate circuit performance.

Answer:

The RC model directly impacts:

  • Switching delay: Higher R or C increases the RC time constant (τ = RC), leading to slower transitions.
  • Signal integrity: Capacitance causes slow rise/fall times, and resistance can lead to voltage drops.
  • Power consumption: Especially dynamic power, since P_dynamic ∝ C × V² × f.

Answer:

• Capacitive components:

  • Gate-to-source capacitance (Cgs)
  • Gate-to-drain capacitance (Cgd)
  • Gate-to-bulk/body (Cgb)
  • Drain-to-bulk and source-to-bulk junction capacitances (Cdb, Csb)

• Resistive components:

  • Channel resistance (Rch)
  • Source/drain resistance (Rs, Rd)
  • Interconnect resistance

Answer:

Parasitic capacitances arise due to:

  • Overlaps between gate and source/drain (Cgd, Cgs)
  • Junction capacitance at the source/drain-substrate interface (Csb, Cdb)
  • Gate oxide capacitance (Cox)

Answer:

Channel resistance (Rch) limits the current drive of the MOSFET and contributes to the overall RC delay. It becomes more significant in short-channel devices, affecting:

  • Drive strength
  • Voltage drop
  • Power dissipation

Answer:

As devices scale:

  • Capacitance decreases due to thinner oxides and smaller gate areas.
  • Resistance increases due to shorter channels and narrower interconnects.

However, interconnect parasitics increase relatively, making wire delays (RC of metal layers) more critical than gate delays in deep submicron and FinFET nodes.

Answer:

The RC delay for a CMOS inverter is estimated using:
τ = Ron × Cload
Where:

  • Ron is the equivalent on-resistance of the PMOS or NMOS (depending on transition)
  • Cload includes intrinsic capacitances + interconnect + fan-out gates

This time constant determines the propagation delay and transition speeds.

Answer:

In advanced nodes:

  • Interconnects dominate delay over transistor switching.
  • RC modeling is used for timing closure, crosstalk analysis, and signal integrity.
  • Tools use distributed RC networks to simulate long wires accurately.

Neglecting interconnect RC can result in failed timing or incorrect signal behavior.

Answer:

• Dynamic power: Increases with higher capacitance (P = αCV²f)
• Static power: Less directly affected, but resistive leakage paths can contribute in scaled devices.
RC modeling helps quantify dynamic losses and optimize power during design.

Answer:

In SPICE:

  • The MOSFET model (e.g., Level 1–3, BSIM) includes intrinsic parasitic R and C.
  • Additional RC elements (e.g., lumped or distributed) can be added explicitly for:
    • Interconnects
    • Substrate coupling
    • Gate resistance (Rg), Source/Drain resistances

Simulation provides waveforms, delay, and power metrics essential for physical design and verification.
